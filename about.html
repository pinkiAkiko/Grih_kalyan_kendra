Dedicated to the welfare of Central Government Employees and their families since 1957.

Welfare & Creation

Grih Kalyan Kendra was set up in the year 1957 under the then Ministry of Home Affairs as a Staff Welfare Scheme with a view to promote the socio-economic and educational status of needy Central Government employees and their family dependents through various welfare activities like Nursery Schools, Creche centre and Craft Centre etc. in their residential colonies in Delhi.

The activities of the Kendra subsequently widened to other cities, viz Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bangalore, Nagpur, Dehradun, Jaipur, Faridabad and Ghaziabad.

In order to instill further confidence in the central government employees and their family dependants and signal the governmentâ€™s commitment to provide stable policy framework for their welfare, the Kendra was registered as a Society named Grih Kalyan Kendra Board (GKK Board) under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, in the year 1965.

It was working under the aegis of Ministry of Home Affairs till year 1986. Since 26th February, 1987, GKK has been working under the aegis of DoPT.
